## Marketing

- **[2026-04-13] Thin ad budgets produce zero data, not "bad results."** $20/day across 100+ keywords gives 1-2 clicks/day. At 3-5% conversion, you need 30-50 clicks for 1 lead. That's 2-3 weeks per lead. Either concentrate budget on 5-10 keywords at $50+/day, or use free channels instead. (Source: Forge — Verse Tech Google Ads)

- **[2026-04-13] Generic outreach gets deleted. Tangible outreach gets read.** "We build custom digital systems" = delete. "I built a website for your company, here it is: [link]" = click. Always include something the recipient can see, touch, or use. (Source: Forge — cold email campaign rewrite)

- **[2026-04-13] Never pay for ads before free channels are proven.** If you can't get 10 customers from Product Hunt / Reddit / Twitter / cold email, paid ads won't fix the problem. They'll just burn money faster. (Source: Forge — $500 ad spend, $5 revenue)

## Product

- **[2026-04-13] Registration walls before payment kill conversion.** Let users pay first (Stripe captures email). Create their account after checkout completes. Every form field between "I want this" and "take my money" loses people. (Source: Forge — Clawback analysis)

- **[2026-04-13] Verify leads before investing time.** A form submission is not a customer. Validate emails before building anything for a specific lead. A fake email cost hours of wasted site-building work. (Source: Forge — Tezla's Cleaning incident)

- **[2026-04-13] Fake social proof gets called out.** Unsubstantiated claims like "92% success rate" erode trust, especially on platforms like Product Hunt and Hacker News where users are skeptical. Use real numbers or no numbers. (Source: Forge — Clawback landing page review)
